Kerala
Gods own Country

Kerala, a lush and verdant state located on the southwestern coast of India, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and progressive social indicators. Often referred to as "God's Own Country," Kerala boasts an array of picturesque landscapes ranging from serene backwaters and palm-fringed beaches to misty mountains and sprawling tea plantations. The state's capital, Thiruvananthapuram, and its bustling commercial hub, Kochi, blend modernity with tradition, offering a vibrant mix of urban and rural experiences. Kerala is also celebrated for its high literacy rates, exemplary healthcare system, and inclusive society, making it a model for social development in India. Its cultural tapestry is enriched with classical art forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, as well as vibrant festivals such as Onam and Thrissur Pooram.
